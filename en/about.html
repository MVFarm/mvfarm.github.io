The Challenges of Traditional Farming

Agriculture is one of humanity’s first instance of mass production and the most vital of all production, providing for the basic necessities of life and fulfilling the lowest levels of Maslow’s Hierarchy of Needs.

Agriculture is however restricted by climate and geography, which in turn fuels rivalries and conflicts between people from villages to nations.

Climate change and mutually-assured destruction by nuclear weapons present the two greatest challenges to humanity’s survival in the 21st Century. Ergo, the survival and prosperity of humanity requires agricultural solutions and policies to bring equality and equity.

MVF products and services bring equal opportunities in agriculture independent of climate and geography. In tandem with government policies, MVF solutions bring equitability to communities worldwide to survive and thrive in the 21st Century.

Introducing Mobile+Vertical Farming

Retired shipping containers retain structural integrity for decades or centuries after losing their sea-worthy certifications and thus provide a cost-effective yet reliable modular structure for agricultural production units.

Shipping containers are easy to transport: pack up and leave within a week or less!

Modular design allows a farm to quickly and easily scale up or down, as well as change configurations to optimize production and minimize waste to become as closed-loop as possible.

Highly-efficient use of energy for ecosystem controls, thus no requirement for large energy sources (like constant connection to electric grid) and no need for energy storage (like batteries)

Energy generation from closed-loop raw materials (i.e. water into hydrogen) and from renewable energy sources (i.e. solar and wind)

Movertical Farm (”MVF”) is the inventor, producer, and operator of climate- and geography-agnostic, resource-efficient, self-sufficient, industrial agricultural production systems. By using standard shipping containers as modules, MVF combines the stability of a sedentary civilisation with the flexibility of a nomadic civilisation.
